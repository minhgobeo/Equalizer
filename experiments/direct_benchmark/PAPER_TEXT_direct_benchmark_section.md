## Subsection: Direct and system-adapted benchmark methods

We further benchmark Algorithm 1 against four recent equalization methods.
For Souza et al. [2024]'s SM-sign-NLMS / VSS-SM-sign-NLMS, the recursion is
implemented exactly as described and run as a direct online adaptive baseline
in the same PAM4 Markov-ISI testbed. For Cui et al. [2024], Dolatsara
[2023], and Chen et al. [2025] we provide **system-adapted** implementations:
each captures the principal idea of the original work — offline tree-ensemble
classification for Cui, constrained Tx-FIR optimization for Dolatsara, and
joint single-pulse FFE/DFE design for Chen — but uses our channel, our
modulation, our slicer, and our test protocol. We do not claim faithful
reproduction; the goal is to test whether their *equalization principles*
transfer to the online decision-directed Markov-tracking regime studied here.

The full pipelines are:

* **Cui-style (adapted)**: features
  $[r(n), r(n-1), r(n-2), r(n-3), r(n)-r(n-1)]$ are extracted from the
  Markov-ISI training block; a bagged tree ensemble is trained offline (with
  a deterministic nearest-centroid fallback when the tree-ensemble toolbox
  is unavailable). The trained classifier is then frozen and applied to
  test data.
* **Dolatsara-style (adapted)**: a Tx-FIR with $\sum_i |w_i| = 1$ is
  optimized by SCBO-lite (constrained random search with Bayesian-style
  bookkeeping) on the realistic Markov training block, then applied as a
  fixed pre-distortion at test time with a fixed Rx slicer.
* **Chen-style (adapted)**: nominal pulse response
  $h_{\mathrm{nom}} = [1, \bar h_2]$ uses the centre Markov state. A
  regularised LS FFE inverts the pulse at a chosen delay and the trailing
  taps of the resulting cascade are used as fixed DFE taps. The receiver
  is non-adaptive at test time.

All four baselines are evaluated alongside Algorithm 1 in both the severe
and the realistic Markov regimes. Table X summarizes pre-FEC BER at
SNR $= 22$ dB.

These adapted baselines optimize equalization settings or classify received
sequences under fixed trained models. In contrast, the present work studies
an online DD adaptive recursion whose reference is generated by the receiver
itself and whose ISI state evolves as a Markov chain. The advantage of
Algorithm 1 is therefore expected — and observed — in the severe Markov
regime. Static or offline-trained equalizers are still competitive in the
realistic regime, where the channel is closer to time-invariant.

## Subsection paragraph: caveat

We emphasize that the ExtraTrees-HMM, SCBO-Tx, and single-pulse FFE/DFE
implementations above are *system-adapted* baselines. They are not exact
reproductions of the cited papers, which were optimized for different
modulation formats, different channel families, and different test
protocols. Our adapted versions allow a like-for-like BER comparison inside
this paper's Markov-DD testbed; the reader should consult the original works
for their authors' intended deployment regimes.
